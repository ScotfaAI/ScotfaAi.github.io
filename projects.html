Exploring the Intersection of AI and Microscopy at the University of Warwick

CelFDrive: AI-assisted microscopy for automated detection of rare events

CelFDrive semi-automates live high-resolution 4D imaging of cells of interest on Lattice LightSheet, integrating deep learning cell classification and localisation from low resolution widefield images. CelFDrive enables efficient detection of rare events in large cell populations, such as late prophase and early prometaphase, and subsequent rapid switching to 4D Lattice LightSheet imaging. We show an increase in the speed of finding cells of interest by a factor of ~20 verses expert users, futhermore this serves to help democratacise access to less technical microscope users.

CelFDrive fully automates high-resolution 4D live and imaging of cells using other microscopes including Spinning Disk Confocal. Instead of switching imaging modes it is able to switch objectives to a higher magnification. In fixed imaging a whole coverslip can be scanned and cells of interest are imaged with custom parameters based on cell state. This allows users to surveil a whole coverslip, saving users time that can be spent on other work.

CelFDrive has been developed in collaboration with Intelligent Imaging Innovations (3i), manufacturer of microscopes with integrated software control through SlideBook™ as part of a Medical Research Council iCASE Studentship. CelFDrive can be easily deployed to any 3i LLSM and 3i Marianas SDC.

CelFDrive is free to use for non-comercial purposes, check out the GitHub

Authors: Scott Brooks, Sara Toral Perez, David S. Corcoran, Karl Kilburn, Hella Broughton, Nigel J. Burroughs, Andrew D. McAinsh, Till Bretschneider
