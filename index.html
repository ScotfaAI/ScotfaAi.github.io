Exploring the Intersection of AI and Microscopy at the University of Warwick

About Me

I am a PhD student in Interdisciplinary Biomedical Sciences at the University of Warwick, focusing on the innovative integration of Artificial Intelligence with Microscopy. My work aims to automate complex imaging processes, enhancing research capabilities and accuracy in biological studies.

I work with a variety of flourescence microscopy methods, particularly the Spinning Disk Confocal and Lattice LightSheet. See my projects page to learn more!

Open To Opportunities

I am interested in working in engaging projects in the Computational Microscopy field, particularly those in the automation/analysis of Biomedical image data both in academia and in industry. I wish to persue opportunities where I can be learning something new almost every day.

I am currently moslty working in Python at the moment, but am learning C++ in my spare time. During my MEng in Computer Science and PhD, I have used a variety of other languages and frameworks.
